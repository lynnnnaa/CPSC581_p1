BOOMA Bouncing Ball

The objective of this project is to create an audio-input interface that does not rely on verbal commands and aims to be delightful.

The idea of the BOOMA Bouncing Ball game was inspired by one of my favorite childhood games Breakout Clone but without the clone. You will have to use your voice to control the movement of the paddle. A LOW volume HIGH pitch voice to move the paddle to the left. A LOW volume LOW pitch voice to move the paddle to the right. People naturally use “a” sounds to perform. Once you catch the ball once, the ball will switch to a random colour from red, blue and black. Then you will have to use a HIGH volume voice to switch the colour of the paddle in order to match the ball’s colour.

Your score shows on the top left corner, each time you catch a ball with the matching colour, you will get a point. Your remaining life shows no in the top right corner. You have three lives in total.
